# Purpose

The `/12-Research` directory serves as the long-term research space for Project NOAH.

Unlike the documents in `/01-Project`, which define the project's philosophy, architecture, roadmap, and engineering decisions, this directory is dedicated to exploring ideas that may shape the future of Project NOAH.

Research topics range from practical implementation concepts to ambitious long-term investigations.

Some research may eventually become architectural decisions.

Some may remain active research for years.

Others may prove impossible.

Every research topic contributes to the long-term evolution of Project NOAH.

---

# Research Philosophy

Research begins with curiosity.

Not every valuable idea can be implemented today.

Some concepts require new technologies.

Some require scientific breakthroughs.

Some may never become reality.

Project NOAH embraces uncertainty as part of the research process.

Ideas are documented not because they are guaranteed to succeed, but because they are worth exploring.

# Research Principles

Research within Project NOAH follows several principles.

- Curiosity over certainty.
- Understanding over complexity.
- Long-term thinking over short-term optimization.
- Exploration over assumption.
- Continuous refinement over perfection.
- Evidence over speculation whenever possible.

Research documents should remain open to revision as knowledge evolves.

---

# Research Lifecycle

Research within Project NOAH generally follows this lifecycle.

```
Idea
    ↓
Research
    ↓
Experiment
    ↓
Evaluation
    ↓
Architecture Candidate
    ↓
Decision
    ↓
Implementation
```

Not every research topic will reach implementation.

Some research may remain open indefinitely.

# Document Structure

Although every research document is unique, the following structure is recommended whenever applicable.

```
Purpose

Motivation

Research Areas

Current Challenges

Possible Architectures

Related Technologies

Open Questions

References

Related Documents
```

Not every document requires every section.

Research documents should prioritize clarity over rigid formatting.
